About

I am a final-year undergraduate at Nanyang Normal University, China. Since 2024, I have been working independently on a single research programme: the statistical physics of stochastic dynamics on constrained state spaces, with the probability simplex as the central object.

The three papers below represent successive stages of one evolving line of inquiry — from spectral stability and network geometry (Paper 1), to fluctuation suppression mechanisms and finite-size scaling (Paper 2), to the discovery of a geometry-induced anomalous scaling bound η < 1/2 and its universality (Paper 3). All three were completed independently as sole author.

Research Interests
01 Nonequilibrium statistical physics of stochastic dynamics on constrained state spaces
02 Fluctuation scaling and finite-size analysis; anomalous scaling laws and universal bounds
03 Diversity stabilization in networked competitive systems; stochastic replicator dynamics
04 Large deviation theory on compact manifolds; path-space statistics
05 Applications to evolutionary game theory and multi-agent learning dynamics
